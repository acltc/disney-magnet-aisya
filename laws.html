Illinois Compiled Statutes Chapter 510. Animals Act 70. Humane Care For Animals Act

ยง 510 ILCS 70/3.01. Cruel treatment

-Sec. 3.01. Cruel treatment. No person or owner may beat, cruelly treat, torment, starve, overwork or otherwise abuse any animal. No owner may abandon any animal where it may become a public charge or may suffer injury, hunger or exposure.

-If found guilty of committing this crime, a person will be charged with a Class A misdemeanor. If found guilty a second time, a person with be charged with a Class 4 felony. Also, psychological or psychiatric treatment may also be assigned.

ยง 510 ILCS 70/3.02. Aggravated cruelty

-Sec. 3.02. Aggravated cruelty. No person may intentionally commit an act that causes a companion animal to suffer serious injury or death. Aggravated cruelty does not include euthanasia of a companion animal through recognized methods approved by the Department of Agriculture.

-If found guilty of committing this crime, a person will be charged with a Class 4 felony. If found guilty a second time, a person with be charged with a Class 3 felony. Also, psychological or psychiatric treatment may also be assigned.

ยง 510 ILCS 70/3.03. Animal torture

-Sec. 3.03. Animal torture. (a) A person commits animal torture when that person without legal justification knowingly or intentionally tortures an animal. For purposes of this Section, and subject to subsection (b), "torture" means infliction of or subjection to extreme physical pain, motivated by an intent to increase or prolong the pain, suffering, or agony of the animal.

-NOTE: Fishing, hunting, and other unintentional harm to animals may not be considered animal abuse.

-If found guilty of committing this crime, a person will be charged with a Class 3 felony. Also, psychological or psychiatric treatment may also be assigned.
